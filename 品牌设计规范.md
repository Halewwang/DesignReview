# EMKE — Style Reference / Design Specification

> Version: Draft v0.1  
> Source: EMKE Brand Positioning 1.0, EMKE Visual Identity & Brand Guidelines V2.1, EMKE Amazon Bathroom Mirror Product Details Guidelines V1.0  
> Intended use: Website, Amazon PDP/A+ content, social media, paid ads, product detail pages, and AI-assisted design/code generation.

---

## 1. Style Essence

**Theme:** warm-minimal, rational, clean, accessible premium  
**Style phrase:** *Practical European bathroom clarity*

EMKE’s visual language should feel calm, transparent, engineered, and easy to trust. It should not look like an over-luxury bathroom brand, nor like a low-cost Amazon commodity. The right position is **mid-to-high quality with rational value**: clean German-influenced order, practical product clarity, restrained warmth, and enough human softness to make bathroom products feel approachable.

The brand should express:

- **Reliable engineering** — precise layout, clear information hierarchy, functional product proof.
- **Accessible quality** — premium but not inflated; refined but not cold.
- **Warm minimalism** — neutral backgrounds, natural material tones, soft light, restrained accent color.
- **Transparent decision support** — direct specifications, visible benefits, easy comparison, low ambiguity.
- **Human-centered bathroom solutions** — products are shown as solutions to daily bathroom pain points, not isolated hardware.

Avoid making EMKE look like a luxury hotel brand with excessive gold, marble, dramatic contrast, or ornamental styling. EMKE should feel like a well-designed European home improvement brand: rational, clean, warm, and practical.

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Mission

**Chinese:** 用智慧设计破解生活难题，让品质卫浴触手可及。  
**English:** Redefine the bathroom experience through smart design, making quality bathroom solutions accessible to everyday homes.

### Vision

To become one of the most trusted mid-to-high value-for-money bathroom brands in Europe, recognized as a strong balance between quality and price, and a first-consideration brand for rational consumers when purchasing bathroom products.

### Market Position

EMKE is positioned as a bathroom specialist combining:

- German-inspired precision
- Minimalist aesthetics
- Reliable product quality
- Reasonable and transparent pricing
- Functional, human-centered design

### Target Audience

**Core audience:**  
30–55-year-old middle-income households, first-time homeowners, and home renovators with limited budgets but clear expectations for quality.

**Secondary audience:**  
25–35-year-old young professionals and renters willing to invest in practical upgrades that improve everyday living quality.

### Brand Personality

EMKE should sound and look:

- **Precise and reliable**
- **Rational and pragmatic**
- **Clear and transparent**
- **Friendly and inclusive**

### Communication Style

Use direct, factual, product-solution language. The tone should be professional without being obscure, friendly without being casual, and benefit-led without exaggeration.

**Do:**

- Lead with the actual user benefit.
- Explain product features through practical scenarios.
- Use measurable specifications where available.
- Help the user make decisions quickly.
- Use clear structure and short paragraphs.

**Don’t:**

- Over-romanticize bathroom products.
- Use vague premium language such as “ultimate luxury” without proof.
- Overload the page with technical jargon.
- Use aggressive sales language.
- Hide product information behind decorative copy.

## 3. Design Principles

### 3.1 Rational Order

All layouts should be grid-based and information-led. Alignment, spacing, and hierarchy matter more than decorative effects. EMKE should look organized before it looks expressive.

### 3.2 Warm Utility

The design should remain minimal, but not sterile. Warm neutrals, light wood, beige surfaces, soft grey, and natural lighting help bathroom products feel usable and residential.

### 3.3 Visible Quality

Product surfaces, materials, functional details, and installation logic should be clearly shown. Avoid excessive atmosphere that hides product form, scale, or usability.

### 3.4 Accessible Premium

The brand should communicate quality without relying on luxury clichés. Premium cues should come from restraint, clarity, material realism, and consistency.

### 3.5 Decision Clarity

Every product page, banner, or ad should answer:

1. What is this product?
2. What problem does it solve?
3. Why is it reliable?
4. What feature should the user remember?
5. What action should the user take next?

- Use warm supporting colors for lifestyle and bathroom scenes.
- Keep total accent color usage under approximately 10–15% of the visual area.

**Don’t:**

- Use teal as a large full-page background unless it is a brand divider or special campaign section.
- Use saturated colors that compete with bathroom products.
- Use black-heavy or neon-heavy UI treatments.
- Use gold/marble luxury palettes as the default brand direction.
- Mix too many supporting colors in one layout.

## 5. Typography Tokens

### 5.1 Primary Typeface

**Font:** Inter  
**Role:** Main website, product pages, UI, body copy, navigation, product information, Amazon content.

| Token | Size | Weight | Line Height | Role |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| `--type-display` | 50px | 600 | 1.08 | Website hero title / campaign title |
| `--type-heading-lg` | 40px | 600 | 1.15 | Section heading |
| `--type-heading-md` | 22px | 500 | 1.25 | Product module heading / card title |
| `--type-body-lg` | 24px | 300 | 1.45 | Large descriptive copy |
| `--type-body` | 20px | 300 | 1.45 | Product name / secondary body |
| `--type-price` | 20px | 500 | 1.2 | Product price |
| `--type-button` | 24px | 400 | 1.2 | CTA button |
| `--type-nav` | 16px | 600 | 1.2 | Top navigation / category label |

### 5.2 Numeric Typeface

**Font:** Arvo  
**Role:** Price, percentage, date, promotion figures, numeric badges.

Use Arvo when numbers must become a visual anchor, such as:

- `49.99€`
- `50%`
- `2025`
- `7/16`

Do not use Arvo for long text blocks.

### 5.3 Typeface Conflict to Resolve

The brand positioning document references **Nunito Sans**, while the visual identity and Amazon guidelines use **Inter** and **Arvo**. For production consistency, use:

- **Inter** as the primary brand/system font.
- **Arvo** for numbers, price, percentage, and date emphasis.
- Do not introduce Nunito Sans into new UI or marketing templates unless the brand team formally decides to replace Inter.

This is important. If an AI coding agent sees both font systems, it may mix them randomly and destroy consistency.

## 6. Logo System

### 6.1 Logo Character

The EMKE logo is simple, elegant, and internationally oriented. The updated wordmark should feel visually balanced, mature, and durable.

### 6.2 Clear Space

Use the height of the letter **“E”** as the base clear-space unit `x`.

- No text, icon, image, button, or edge should enter the `x` clear-space area.
- Keep consistent clear space across web, print, Amazon, and social placements.

### 6.3 Minimum Size

- Digital minimum width: `57px`
- Print minimum width: `20mm`

Do not use the logo below these dimensions.

### 6.4 Logo Color Modes

| Mode | Usage |
|---|---|
| Positive / ink logo | Light, low-complexity backgrounds. |
| Reversed / white logo | Dark backgrounds or brand-color backgrounds. |
| Single-color logo | Monochrome or limited-print applications. |

### 6.5 Background Control

When placing logo on images:

- Ensure strong luminance and color contrast.
- Use positive logo on light backgrounds.
- Use reversed logo on dark backgrounds.
- Use single-color or reversed logo on teal/brand-color backgrounds.
- Avoid complex image areas behind the logo.

### 6.6 Incorrect Usage

Do not:

- Add unapproved colors.
- Stretch or distort the logo.
- Change the full logo color arbitrarily.
- Add outlines.
- Add decorative elements.
- Change the logo structure or proportions.

## 7. Layout System

### 7.1 Website Grid

**Grid:** 20-column centered layout  
**Column width:** 56px  
**Gutter:** 28px  
**Offset:** 0  
**Recommended usage:** 2-column, 3-column, and 4-column layouts depending on content density.

Use the grid to create a clean, engineered, and predictable structure. EMKE layouts should not feel randomly composed.

### 7.2 Amazon PC Grid

**Grid:** 24-column centered layout  
**Column width:** 38px  
**Gutter:** 24px  
**Offset:** 0  
**Recommended usage:** 2-column, 3-column, and 4-column PDP/A+ modules.

### 7.3 Amazon Main Image Grid

**Grid:** 12-column centered layout  
**Column width:** 92px  
**Gutter:** 45px  
**Offset:** 0  
**Recommended usage:** Main product image, feature bullets, visual callout overlays.

### 7.4 Spatial Rhythm

Recommended production tokens:

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| `--space-2xs` | 4px | Micro gaps, icon-label gap |
| `--space-xs` | 8px | Tag inner spacing, small gaps |
| `--space-sm` | 10px | Tag/headline local gap |
| `--space-md` | 20px | Text group gap, title-body gap |
| `--space-lg` | 24px | Image module gap |
| `--space-xl` | 30px | Body-to-tag gap / CTA gap |
| `--space-2xl` | 60px | Banner bottom/top safe area |
| `--space-3xl` | 62px | Banner side safe area |
| `--space-4xl` | 80px | Large section padding |
| `--space-5xl` | 100px | Main-image safe area |

The Amazon files repeatedly use 60px, 62px, 20px, 24px, 30px, 92px, and 100px spacing logic. Normalize these into tokens rather than letting designers create one-off measurements.

## 8. Shape, Surface, and Elevation

The source guidelines do not define border radius and shadow tokens explicitly. Use the following as a production recommendation.

### Border Radius

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---:|---|
| `--radius-none` | 0px | Technical grids, strict image masks |
| `--radius-sm` | 4px | Tags, small UI controls |
| `--radius-md` | 8px | Cards, product tiles, inputs |
| `--radius-lg` | 12px | Larger content modules |
| `--radius-pill` | 999px | CTA pills, badges, feature tags |

### Shadows

EMKE should avoid heavy, dramatic shadows. Use low-opacity shadows only to create separation on light backgrounds.

| Token | Value | Role |
|---|---|---|
| `--shadow-subtle` | `0 2px 8px rgba(35,24,21,0.06)` | Product cards / light elevation |
| `--shadow-card` | `0 8px 24px rgba(35,24,21,0.08)` | PDP modules, Amazon banners |
| `--shadow-floating` | `0 16px 40px rgba(35,24,21,0.10)` | Modal or hero card only |

**Do not use glossy, high-contrast, gaming-style, or SaaS-style shadows.**

## 9. Image Direction

### 9.1 Overall Image Mood

EMKE imagery should feel:

- Clean
- Residential
- Warm
- Practical
- Bright but not overexposed
- Premium through material clarity, not luxury staging

Preferred atmosphere:

- Soft daylight
- Warm beige or off-white walls
- Light wood
- Matte surfaces
- Clear glass
- Realistic bathroom proportions
- Spacious negative space

### 9.2 Product Photography Rules

**Do:**

- Show product structure clearly.
- Maintain accurate shape, scale, and material.
- Use clean backgrounds and controlled reflections.
- Include functional detail close-ups when helpful.
- Use environment images to explain use, installation, and daily value.

**Don’t:**

- Hide the product behind excessive styling.
- Use overly complex tile patterns behind logo or product labels.
- Use dramatic luxury hotel lighting as the default.
- Use fake reflections that reduce product clarity.
- Overuse plants, candles, towels, or decorative props.

### 9.3 Scene Direction Keywords

Use these keywords when generating or selecting EMKE imagery:

- warm minimal bathroom
- German-inspired functional clarity
- soft daylight
- matte beige wall
- off-white ceramic
- light oak wood
- brushed metal detail
- clear glass shower screen
- organized bathroom space
- human-centered daily routine
- clean product hero shot
- accessible premium

### 9.4 Amazon Image Direction

Amazon images must prioritize decision clarity over brand atmosphere.

Required image types:

1. Main product image
2. Product-in-room image
3. Feature callout image
4. Size/dimension image
5. Installation or assembly logic
6. Material/detail close-up
7. Functional comparison image
8. Usage scenario image

### 10.2 Main Image Typography

**Font:** Inter

| Level | Size | Weight | Line Height | Role |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Main title | 90px | 600 / 400 | 92px | Core product claim |
| Description | 34px | 300 | 44px | Supporting explanation |
| Feature text | 30px | 300 | 34px | Feature callout |
| Auxiliary text | 28px | 300 | 30px | Secondary technical information |

### 10.3 Amazon Banner Layout Logic

Common banner behavior:

- Use 1464px-wide PC banners where applicable.
- Keep 60–62px safe margins for text-heavy PDP modules.
- Use 20–30px vertical rhythm between tag, headline, description, and feature labels.
- Right-side text blocks should align to a clear internal grid and avoid exceeding the defined text width.
- Product feature modules may use 2-column, 3-column, or 4-column structures.
- Keep text blocks compact. Amazon viewers scan; do not make them read paragraphs.

### 10.4 Amazon Copy Pattern

Use this order:

1. Feature headline
2. One-line benefit
3. Proof/specification
4. Visual callout or icon
5. Optional tag

Example structure:

```text
Stepless Dimmable Light
Adjust brightness smoothly for different daily routines.
10%–100% brightness range · Memory function
```

## 11. Web UI Direction

### 11.1 Page Structure

Recommended hierarchy:

1. Hero: one core promise + one product/scene image
2. Product category cards
3. Feature proof section
4. Scenario/lifestyle section
5. Comparison or decision-support module
6. Reviews / trust proof
7. CTA / product navigation

### 11.2 Web Component Style

| Component | Direction |
|---|---|
| Header | Clean, lightweight, strong alignment, no visual noise. |
| CTA button | Pill or soft rectangle, teal or ink depending on background. |
| Product card | White or mist background, product-first, restrained text. |
| Feature tag | Small rounded label, teal or pale neutral. |
| Comparison table | High clarity, low decoration, clear typography. |
| Section divider | Thin line or soft background shift, not heavy gradient. |

### 11.3 UI States

| State | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Default | Neutral surface, ink text, low emphasis. |
| Hover | Slight tint, subtle underline, or 4–6% background shift. |
| Active | Teal accent, clear indicator. |
| Disabled | Pale steel background, body grey text. |
| Error | Terracotta signal, restrained use. |
| Success | Olive utility, restrained use. |

## 12. Social & Paid Media Direction

### 12.1 General Social Layout

Social layouts should preserve the same rational grid logic as website and Amazon content, but may increase visual contrast for scroll-stopping.

Use:

- 1:1 Instagram layouts
- Pinterest vertical layouts
- 16:9 campaign/poster layouts

### 12.2 Poster Rules

- Keep the logo in a consistent location within the same campaign family.
- Use the same logo placement rule across all related assets.
- Use grid-based text blocks rather than freeform collage.
- Product or scene image should dominate; text should clarify, not decorate.
- Avoid excessive badges, price explosions, or cluttered promotional stickers.

### 12.3 Social Copy Style

Use practical benefit hooks:

- “Designed for everyday clarity.”
- “A cleaner bathroom routine starts here.”
- “Smart storage, calmer mornings.”
- “Reliable details for daily use.”
- “Quality bathroom solutions without overpaying.”

## 13. Content Tone

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Clear | Every sentence should be easy to understand. |
| Factual | Claims should be supported by specs, function, or use case. |
| Helpful | Copy should help a buyer choose. |
| Calm | Avoid aggressive promotional pressure. |
| Practical | Link design features to daily value. |

### Recommended Sentence Pattern

```text
[Feature] helps [user] achieve [practical result] in [daily scenario].
```

Example:

```text
The anti-fog mirror keeps reflections clear after a hot shower, helping you get ready without wiping the glass repeatedly.
```

### Copy Do / Don’t

**Do:**

- “Easy to install with clear mounting instructions.”
- “Designed for everyday bathroom routines.”
- “Soft LED lighting supports morning and evening use.”
- “A practical upgrade for compact bathrooms.”

**Don’t:**

- “The ultimate luxury bathroom masterpiece.”
- “Unbeatable revolutionary design.”
- “A magical experience beyond imagination.”
- “Perfect for everyone in every situation.”

## 14. Accessibility & Readability

Minimum expectations:

- Body text contrast should pass WCAG AA where possible.
- Do not place text over busy bathroom scenes without overlay control.
- Avoid thin light-grey text below 16px.
- Amazon feature text must remain legible on mobile after compression.
- CTA states must be visually distinguishable from normal text.
- Do not use teal text on low-contrast backgrounds.

## 19. Do / Don’t Summary

### Do

- Use clean, warm, neutral bathroom environments.
- Use strict grid alignment.
- Keep information direct and product-led.
- Use teal as a restrained brand accent.
- Use Inter consistently.
- Use Arvo for numeric emphasis only.
- Show functional product proof clearly.
- Use soft shadows, subtle lines, and calm spacing.
- Keep Amazon banners scannable and benefit-led.

### Don’t

- Mix Nunito, Inter, and Arvo randomly.
- Overuse teal as a full-page background.
- Add decorative colors to the logo.
- Place logo on complex image backgrounds.
- Use excessive luxury cues like heavy marble, gold, and dramatic dark interiors.
- Use generic SaaS UI styling.
- Overload Amazon images with text.
- Let atmosphere hide product clarity.
- Make unsupported claims.

## 20. Open Issues Before Finalizing

1. **Font system conflict:** confirm whether Inter replaces Nunito Sans fully.
2. **Auxiliary color correction:** confirm whether RGB `175,153,113` should be `#AF9971`.
3. **Border radius:** not defined in source guidelines; proposed values need approval.
4. **Shadow/elevation:** not defined in source guidelines; proposed values need approval.
5. **Icon style:** source files show icons but do not define stroke, corner, or fill rules.
6. **Photography grading:** image mood is inferential from brand visuals and should be converted into a formal photo guide.
7. **Component states:** hover, active, disabled, error, success states are proposed for digital implementation.
8. **Mobile website typography:** not fully specified; needs a responsive type scale.
9. **Amazon mobile layout:** the file includes MB sections, but final production should extract exact mobile-safe typography and image ratios into a separate spec.
10. **Design token ownership:** decide whether tokens live in Figma variables, Tailwind config, CSS variables, or all three.
